# Ad Creative

Design scroll-stopping ad creative that captures attention and drives action.

## When to Use
- New paid ad campaigns
- Creative fatigue/declining performance
- Testing new messaging angles
- Seasonal/promotional campaigns
- Launching on new platforms

## Don't Use When
- You don't know your audience
- The offer isn't validated
- You're using stock photos without customization
- You can't maintain consistency with landing page

## Creative Principles

### 1. Stop the Scroll
- Bold, contrasting colors
- Movement (video, animation)
- Pattern interrupt (unexpected imagery)
- Faces and emotions (eye contact)
- Text overlays that demand attention

### 2. Platform-Native
- Match organic content style
- Use platform-specific formats
- Vertical video for mobile
- Sound-off optimization (captions)

### 3. Clear Message
- One idea per creative
- Benefit in first 3 seconds (video)
- Readable text (max 20% text on image for Meta)
- Strong visual hierarchy

## Creative Formats

### Static Images
**Best for:** Simple messages, brand awareness, retargeting

**Types:**
- **Product shots:** Clean, high-quality product images
- **Lifestyle:** Product in use, aspirational
- **User-generated:** Authentic customer photos
- **Text/graphic:** Quotes, stats, testimonials
- **Comparison:** Before/after, vs competitor

**Specs:**
- Meta: 1080x1080 (square), 1080x1920 (story)
- LinkedIn: 1200x627
- Twitter: 1200x675
- File size: <8MB

### Video Ads
**Best for:** Storytelling, demos, emotional connection

**Types:**
- **Explainer:** How it works
- **Demo:** Product in action
- **Testimonial:** Customer stories
- **UGC:** User-generated style
- **B-roll:** Lifestyle footage

**Specs:**
- Length: 15-30 seconds (feed), 6 seconds (bumper)
- Aspect ratios: 16:9 (landscape), 1:1 (square), 9:16 (vertical)
- Captions: Always
- Hook: First 3 seconds

### Carousel Ads
**Best for:** Multiple products, storytelling, features

**Best practices:**
- First card is the hook
- Logical flow between cards
- Each card can have unique link
- 3-5 cards optimal

### Collection Ads (Meta)
**Best for:** Ecommerce, product catalogs

**Structure:**
- Cover image/video
- 4 product images below
- Instant experience or website link

## Creative Angles to Test

### Problem-Solution
- Show the pain point
- Transition to your solution
- Clear before/after

### Social Proof
- Customer testimonial
- Star ratings/reviews
- User count ("Join 10,000+")
- Media logos

### Offer-Focused
- Discount percentage
- Free trial/bonus
- Limited time urgency
- Risk reversal (guarantee)

### Educational
- Quick tip or hack
- Myth-busting
- "How to" preview
- Curiosity gap

### Emotional
- Fear (missing out, falling behind)
- Aspiration (lifestyle, success)
- Belonging (community, tribe)
- Relief (problem solved)

## Design Best Practices

### Color
- Brand colors (consistency)
- Contrast for readability
- Color psychology (red=urgency, blue=trust)

### Typography
- Maximum 2 fonts
- Large, readable sizes
- High contrast on background
- Short phrases, not paragraphs

### Composition
- Rule of thirds
- Clear focal point
- Visual hierarchy
- Safe zones for text (center often obscured)

## Video Production Tips

### Low-Budget Options
- Smartphone + good lighting
- Screen recordings (SaaS)
- Stock footage + text overlays
- Canva/PowerPoint animations

### UGC Style
- Filmed on phone
- Natural lighting
- Direct address to camera
- Authentic, not polished

### Editing
- Fast cuts (every 2-3 seconds)
- Captions throughout
- Music (royalty-free)
- End card with CTA

## Creative Testing Framework

### Test Variables (one at a time)
1. **Hook/Opening** — First 3 seconds
2. **Visual style** — Photography vs illustration
3. **Value prop angle** — Feature vs benefit
4. **CTA** — Different action words
5. **Format** — Image vs video vs carousel

### Testing Structure
- 3-5 variations per ad set
- Let each get 1000+ impressions
- Kill <1% CTR quickly
- Scale >2% CTR

## Creative Fatigue Management

**Signs:**
- Rising CPM/CPA
- Falling CTR
- High frequency (>3)

**Solutions:**
- Refresh creative every 2-4 weeks
- Rotate variations
- Test new angles
- Update for seasonality
