# Content Strategy

Develop strategic content plans that align with business goals and audience needs.

## When to Use
- Launching or revamping content marketing
- Entering new markets or audiences
- Aligning content with business goals
- Building sustainable content operations

## Don't Use When
- You need tactical execution only (use specific skills)
- You can't commit to consistent publishing
- You're looking for quick wins without long-term investment

## Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Define Goals
**Business objectives → Content objectives**

| Business Goal | Content Objective |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Increase brand awareness | Reach, impressions, share of voice |
| Generate leads | Email signups, content downloads |
| Drive sales | Product content, case studies |
| Reduce support tickets | Help docs, tutorials, FAQs |
| Build authority | Thought leadership, research |

### Step 2: Audience Research

**Create Audience Personas:**
- Demographics (role, company size, industry)
- Psychographics (challenges, goals, fears)
- Content preferences (formats, channels, topics)
- Buying stage awareness

**Map Content to Journey:**

| Stage | Awareness | Consideration | Decision |
|-------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| **Mindset** | "I have a problem" | "What are my options?" | "Why you vs them?" |
| **Content Types** | Educational blog, guides | Comparison posts, webinars | Case studies, demos |
| **CTA** | Subscribe, download | Trial, consultation | Purchase, contract |

### Step 3: Content Audit

**Inventory:**
- List all existing content
- Performance metrics (traffic, engagement, conversions)
- Content gaps vs competitors
- Outdated content needing refresh

**Categorize:**
- Keep (performing well)
- Update (needs refresh)
- Consolidate (duplicate/thin)
- Remove (irrelevant/outdated)

### Step 4: Topic & Keyword Strategy

**Pillar-Cluster Model:**
1. **Pillar page** — Broad topic (2,000-3,000 words)
2. **Cluster content** — Specific subtopics linking to pillar
3. **Internal linking** — Connect related content

**Topic Selection Criteria:**
- Search volume/demand
- Business relevance
- Competitive difficulty
- Your unique angle/expertise

### Step 5: Content Mix

**Content Types:**
- **Evergreen** — Always relevant (guides, how-tos)
- **Timely** — News, trends, seasonality
- **Repurposed** — One asset → multiple formats
- **Interactive** — Tools, calculators, quizzes
- **User-generated** — Customer stories, reviews

**Content Formats by Funnel Stage:**
- **Top:** Blog posts, videos, infographics, social
- **Middle:** Ebooks, webinars, whitepapers, case studies
- **Bottom:** Demos, trials, consultations, pricing pages

### Step 6: Editorial Calendar

**Monthly Planning:**
- Themes or campaigns
- Pillar content priorities
- Evergreen content production
- Content refreshes

**Weekly Execution:**
- Specific pieces to publish
- Distribution across channels
- Promotion schedule

**Calendar Fields:**
- Title/topic
- Author
- Format
- Channel
- Due date
- Publish date
- Status
- Distribution plan

### Step 7: Distribution Strategy

**Owned:**
- Website/blog
- Email newsletter
- Social media
- Community/forum

**Earned:**
- Guest posts
- PR/media coverage
- Influencer partnerships
- SEO/organic search

**Paid:**
- Social ads
- Search ads
- Sponsored content
- Retargeting

## Content Operations

**Workflow:**
1. Ideation → 2. Brief → 3. Create → 4. Edit → 5. Publish → 6. Distribute → 7. Measure → 8. Optimize

**Team Roles:**
- Strategist (planning, measurement)
- Writers (content creation)
- Editor (quality control)
- Designer (visuals)
- Distributor (promotion)

**Tools:**
- Planning: Trello, Asana, Airtable, Notion
- SEO: Ahrefs, SEMrush, Clearscope
- Analytics: Google Analytics, Search Console

## Success Metrics

**Content Performance:**
- Traffic (total, organic, by channel)
- Engagement (time on page, scroll depth)
- Conversions (leads, sales, by content piece)
- SEO (rankings, backlinks)

**Operational:**
- Publishing velocity
- Content ROI
- Team productivity
