# Email Sequence

Create strategic email sequences that nurture relationships and drive conversions at every stage.

## When to Use
- Onboarding new users
- Post-download nurture
- Abandoned cart recovery
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Post-purchase follow-up
- Webinar/event sequences

## Sequence Types

### 1. Welcome Sequence
**Trigger:** New subscriber
**Goal:** Build relationship, set expectations

**Structure:**
1. **Email 1 (Immediate):** Welcome + deliver lead magnet
2. **Email 2 (Day 2):** Story/background, why you do what you do
3. **Email 3 (Day 4):** Best content/resources
4. **Email 4 (Day 7):** Soft pitch or case study
5. **Email 5 (Day 10+):** Regular newsletter cadence

### 2. Nurture Sequence
**Trigger:** Lead magnet download
**Goal:** Educate and move toward sale

**Structure:**
1. **Email 1:** Deliver value + introduce problem
2. **Email 2:** Deep dive on problem
3. **Email 3:** Solution framework (your approach)
4. **Email 4:** Case study/social proof
5. **Email 5:** Direct pitch with offer
6. **Email 6 (if no conversion):** Objection handling
7. **Email 7:** Final call/urgency

### 3. Onboarding Sequence
**Trigger:** New trial signup or purchase
**Goal:** Drive activation/retention

**Structure:**
1. **Email 1 (Immediate):** Welcome + quick win setup
2. **Email 2 (Day 1):** Core feature walkthrough
3. **Email 3 (Day 3):** Use case/example
4. **Email 4 (Day 5):** Power user tip
5. **Email 5 (Day 7):** Check-in/support offer
6. **Email 6 (Day 10):** Case study/ROI proof
7. **Email 7 (Day 14):** Conversion push (trial) or advanced tips (paid)

### 4. Abandoned Cart
**Trigger:** Cart abandonment
**Goal:** Recover lost sales

**Structure:**
1. **Email 1 (1 hour):** Reminder + save cart
2. **Email 2 (24 hours):** Social proof/reviews
3. **Email 3 (72 hours):** Incentive (discount or bonus)
4. **Email 4 (7 days):** Last chance urgency

### 5. Re-engagement
**Trigger:** 30-60 days inactive
**Goal:** Win back or clean list

**Structure:**
1. **Email 1:** "We miss you" + what's new
2. **Email 2:** Best content/resources
3. **Email 3:** Incentive to return
4. **Email 4:** Final notice + unsubscribe option

## Writing Tips for Sequences

**Subject Lines:**
- Vary between curiosity, benefit, and personal
- Avoid repetitive patterns
- Test emoji sparingly

**Email Length:**
- Vary between short and long
- Early emails: shorter, build relationship
- Later emails: can be longer if value is high

**CTAs:**
- One primary CTA per email
- Match to sequence goal
- Progressively more direct as sequence continues

**Personalization:**
- Use first name naturally
- Reference their specific action
- Segment by behavior/interests

## Sequence Optimization

**Track:**
- Open rates by email
- Click rates by email
- Unsubscribe rate (should be <0.5% per email)
- Conversion rate to goal

**A/B Test:**
- Subject lines
- Send times
- CTA copy
- Email length

**Exit Triggers:**
- Converted (stop selling, start onboarding)
- Unsubscribed
- No engagement (move to re-engagement)

## Email Sequence Best Practices

1. **Set expectations upfront** — Tell them what they'll receive and how often
2. **Deliver value in every email** — Even pitch emails should educate
3. **Maintain consistent voice** — Same tone across all emails
4. **Mobile-optimize** — 60%+ read on mobile
5. **Test deliverability** — Check spam scores, authenticate domain
6. **Respect unsubscribe** — Make it easy, honor immediately
