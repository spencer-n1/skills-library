# Color Palette Principles

## Warm Monochrome (Minimalist Style)

**Backgrounds:**
- Pure White: `#FFFFFF`
- Warm Bone/Off-White: `#F7F6F3` or `#FBFBFA`
- Soft Gray: `#F9F9F8`

**Surfaces:**
- Cards: `#FFFFFF` or `#F9F9F8`
- Borders/Dividers: `#EAEAEA` or `rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`

**Text:**
- Primary: `#111111` or `#2F3437` (never pure black)
- Secondary: `#787774` or `#6B7280`
- Muted: `#9CA3AF`

**Accents (Desaturated):**
- Pale Red: `#FDEBEC` (Text: `#9F2F2D`)
- Pale Blue: `#E1F3FE` (Text: `#1F6C9F`)
- Pale Green: `#EDF3EC` (Text: `#346538`)
- Pale Yellow: `#FBF3DB` (Text: `#956400`)

Use accents sparingly — for tags, status badges, or inline highlights only.

## Dark Mode (SolidState Style)

**Backgrounds:**
- Deep Black: `#0a0a0a`
- Dark Charcoal: `#1a1a1a`
- Lighter Dark: `#2a2a2a`

**Text:**
- Primary: `#ffffff`
- Secondary: `#a1a1aa` or `#9ca3af`
- Muted: `#71717a`

**Borders:**
- Subtle: `#27272a` or `#3f3f46`

## Professional/Dark Accents

**For Trades (HVAC, Electricians, etc.):**
- Navy Blue: `#1e3a5f` — Trust, professionalism
- Forest Green: `#2d5016` — Growth, outdoor services
- Deep Red: `#7f1d1d` — Urgency (emergency services)
- Charcoal: `#374151` — Modern, industrial

**Proven Industry Palettes:**
- **Plumbers/Emergency Services:** Dark theme + emergency green (`#19E37A`) — urgency, 24/7 availability
- **Financial Services:** Navy foundation + gold accents — conservative, trustworthy, credentials-focused
- **Healthcare/Physical Therapy:** Sage green (`#5B8C6F`) — soft, healing, approachable
- **Coastal Services (Garage Doors, etc.):** Light blues, sand, beige — location-themed, friendly

**For Luxury/Premium:**
- Gold: `#d4af37` or `#b8860b`
- Deep Navy: `#0f172a`
- Burgundy: `#7c2d12`

## Rules

### Maximum 1 Accent Color
- Saturation should be < 80%
- Use consistently throughout the site

### The "AI Purple/Blue" Ban
- ❌ No neon gradients
- ❌ No purple button glows
- ❌ No electric blue everywhere

### Color Consistency
- Stick to warm OR cool grays — don't mix
- One palette for the entire project
- Use opacity for variations, not new hex codes

### Dark Mode Guidelines
- Never pure `#000000` — use `#0a0a0a` or `#111111`
- Text should never be pure white on pure black (too harsh)
- Use `#f9fafb` or `#f3f4f6` for primary text on dark

## Text Contrast

**Minimum ratios:**
- Normal text: 4.5:1
- Large text: 3:1

**Test with:**
- White text on dark backgrounds
- Dark text on light backgrounds
- Never light gray on white
- Never dark gray on black
