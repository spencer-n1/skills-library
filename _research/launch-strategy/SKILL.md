# Launch Strategy

Plan and execute product launches that create momentum, generate buzz, and drive adoption.

## When to Use
- Launching a new product or feature
- Entering a new market
- Major version updates
- Rebranding or repositioning

## Don't Use When
- Minor updates or bug fixes
- You need stealth/quiet releases
- The product isn't ready

## Launch Framework

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (4-8 weeks before)
**Goals:** Build anticipation, grow waitlist, validate positioning

**Activities:**
- Teaser campaign ("something's coming")
- Landing page with email capture
- Beta/early access program
- Content teasing the problem you solve
- Influencer/press briefings (under NDA)

**Key metrics:** Waitlist signups, email open rates, social engagement

### Phase 2: Launch Week
**Goals:** Maximize awareness, drive initial adoption

**Sequence:**
1. **Day -1:** Press embargo lifts (if applicable)
2. **Day 1:** Announcement (blog, email, social)
3. **Day 2-3:** Deep dives (product tours, demos, AMA)
4. **Day 4-5:** Customer stories, social proof
5. **Day 6-7:** Recap, momentum maintenance

**Channels to coordinate:**
- Email (announcement + sequence)
- Social (platform-native content)
- Blog/product page
- PR/press outreach
- Paid ads (launch-focused)
- Community/forums
- Partnerships/integrations

### Phase 3: Post-Launch (2-4 weeks after)
**Goals:** Sustain momentum, convert interest to usage

**Activities:**
- Onboarding optimization
- Case study development
- Retargeting non-converters
- Feedback collection
- Iteration based on early usage

## Launch Messaging Framework

**1. The Hook (Headline)**
- What it is + why it matters
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Example: "Launch in days, not months"

**2. The Problem** 
- What sucks about current solutions
- Make it relatable and specific

**3. The Solution**
- Your product as the answer
- Key differentiators

**4. Social Proof**
- Beta users, early customers
- Metrics if you have them

**5. The CTA**
- Clear next step
- Reduce friction (no credit card, free trial, etc.)

## Launch Checklist

- [ ] Product is stable and ready
- [ ] Landing page is live and tested
- [ ] Payment/signup flow works end-to-end
- [ ] Support team briefed and staffed
- [ ] Email sequences written and scheduled
- [ ] Social content calendar ready
- [ ] PR kit/assets prepared
- [ ] Analytics and tracking in place
- [ ] Team roles and responsibilities clear
- [ ] Contingency plan for issues

## Launch Angles

| Angle | Best For | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Innovation** | New technology | "First to use AI for X" |
| **Speed** | Faster solutions | "What used to take days, now takes minutes" |
| **Simplicity** | Complex problems | "X without the Y" |
| **Price** | Disruption | "Enterprise features at startup prices" |
| **Values** | Mission-driven | "The ethical alternative to X" |
