# Sales Enablement

Equip sales teams with the content, tools, and training they need to close more deals faster.

## When to Use
- Sales team needs better collateral
- Long sales cycles need acceleration
- New product launches
- Onboarding new sales reps
- Competitive pressure increasing

## Don't Use When
- Product/market fit isn't established
- Sales process isn't defined
- No feedback loop from sales to marketing

## Sales Enablement Framework

### 1. Sales Content Library

**By Funnel Stage:**

**Awareness Stage:**
- Industry reports/research
- Educational webinars
- Thought leadership articles
- ROI calculators

**Consideration Stage:**
- Product demos (live and recorded)
- Case studies by use case
- Comparison guides
- Technical whitepapers

**Decision Stage:**
- Pricing sheets
- Implementation guides
- Security documentation
- Reference customers

**Post-Sale:**
- Onboarding guides
- Success stories
- Expansion playbooks
- Renewal materials

### 2. Battle Cards

**Competitor Battle Card Template:**
```
Competitor: [Name]
Overview: [1-2 sentence positioning]

Strengths:
- [What they do well - be honest]

Weaknesses:
- [Where we win]

Our Differentiators:
- [3 key differences]

Objection Handling:
- "They say X" → "Actually, Y"

Landmines:
- [Questions to ask that expose weaknesses]

Win Stories:
- [2-3 customers who switched from them]
```

### 3. Email Templates

**By Scenario:**
- Cold outreach sequences
- Follow-up after demo
- Proposal follow-up
- Handling objections
- Referral requests

**Template Format:**
- Subject line options
- Email body (customizable sections)
- Expected outcome
- Timing/follow-up

### 4. Call Scripts & Frameworks

**Discovery Call Framework:**
1. **Rapport** (2 min) — Build connection
2. **Agenda** (1 min) — Set expectations
3. **Current State** (10 min) — Understand their situation
4. **Problems** (10 min) — Identify pain points
5. **Desired State** (5 min) — Vision of success
6. **Solution Preview** (5 min) — High-level how we help
7. **Next Steps** (2 min) — Clear path forward

**Key Questions:**
- "What prompted you to look at this now?"
- "How are you handling [problem] today?"
- "What happens if nothing changes?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What does success look like in 6 months?"

### 5. Objection Handling Guide

**Common Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Acknowledge | Reframe | Answer |
|-----------|-------------|---------|--------|
| Too expensive | Budget concerns are valid | Let's look at ROI | Case study showing ROI |
| No time now | Understand priorities | Cost of waiting | Show quick time-to-value |
| Need to think | Decision process | Risk of delay | Remove friction, trial offer |
| Competitor cheaper | Price matters | Total cost of ownership | Value breakdown |
| Not a priority | Current focus | Opportunity cost | Industry trend/consequences |

### 6. Proposal Templates

**Sections:**
1. Executive summary
2. Understanding of their situation
3. Proposed solution
4. Implementation plan
5. Investment/pricing
6. ROI projection
7. Case studies
8. Terms and next steps

## Sales Training

**Onboarding Program:**
- Product deep dive
- Ideal customer profile
- Competitive landscape
- Sales methodology
- Tool training (CRM, etc.)
- Shadowing top performers

**Ongoing Enablement:**
- Weekly win/loss reviews
- Monthly product updates
- Quarterly competitive intel
- Regular role-playing

## Measuring Enablement

**Content Usage:**
- Which assets are used most?
- Which are ignored?
- Time to find assets

**Sales Performance:**
- Win rate
- Sales cycle length
- Deal size
- Quota attainment

**Feedback Loop:**
- Sales confidence in materials
- Requests for new content
- Win/loss reasons

## Sales-Marketing Alignment

**Regular Touchpoints:**
- Weekly sales standup attendance
- Monthly content planning with sales input
- Quarterly strategy reviews
- Win/loss analysis collaboration

**Shared Metrics:**
- MQL to SQL conversion
- Sales accepted leads
- Content-influenced pipeline
- Content-influenced revenue
