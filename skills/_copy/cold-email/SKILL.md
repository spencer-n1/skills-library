# Cold Email

Write cold emails that cut through the noise and start real conversations.

## When to Use
- Prospecting for sales
- Partnership outreach
- PR/media pitching
- Link building
- Investor outreach

## Don't Use When
- You have no clear value proposition
- The recipient has no reason to care
- You're sending mass blasts without personalization
- You're violating spam laws (CAN-SPAM, GDPR)

## Cold Email Principles

1. **It's About Them, Not You** — Lead with their pain, not your product
2. **Brevity Wins** — 50-125 words is the sweet spot
3. **Personalization Signals** — Show you did your research
4. **One Clear Ask** — Don't ask for multiple things
5. **Make It Easy to Say Yes** — Low friction CTAs

## Email Structure

### Subject Line
**Goal:** Get the open

**Formulas:**
- Question about [specific thing]
- [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out
- [Specific result] for [company type]
- Quick question
- [Their company] + [Your company]

**Best practices:**
- 3-5 words ideal
- Avoid spam triggers (Free, Act Now, !!!)
- Create curiosity or relevance
- A/B test variations

### Opening Line
**Goal:** Show this isn't a mass email

**Good examples:**
- "Saw your recent post about [topic]..."
- "Noticed [company] just [news/event]..."
- "Congratulations on [achievement]..."
- "Love what you're doing with [specific thing]..."

**Bad examples:**
- "I hope this email finds you well..."
- "My name is [name] and I work at..."
- "Are you the right person to contact about..."

### Value Proposition
**Goal:** Give them a reason to care

**Format:**
- State a problem they likely have (based on research)
- Briefly mention how you've solved it for similar companies
- Optional: Social proof (logos, results)

**Example:**
"Most [company type] struggle with [pain point]. We helped [similar company] [specific result] in [timeframe]."

### The Ask
**Goal:** Get a response

**Good CTAs:**
- "Worth a brief conversation?"
- "Interested in seeing how this works for [company]?"
- "Open to a quick call next Tuesday or Wednesday?"
- "Worth exploring?"

**Bad CTAs:**
- "Let me know if you're interested"
- "Can we schedule a 30-minute demo?"
- "Here's my calendar link"
- No CTA at all

### Signature
- Name
- Title (optional)
- Company (optional)
- Keep it minimal

## Follow-Up Sequence

**Timing:**
- Email 1: Day 0
- Email 2: Day 3 (bump)
- Email 3: Day 7 (new angle)
- Email 4: Day 14 (breakup)

**Follow-up tactics:**
- **Bump:** Short reply to bring to top of inbox
- **New angle:** Different value proposition
- **Social proof:** Mention new win or relevant news
- **Breakup:** "Should I close your file?"

## Personalization at Scale

**Level 1: Merge Fields**
- Name, company, industry
- Minimum viable personalization

**Level 2: Trigger-Based**
- Recent funding, hiring, news
- Technology they use
- Content they published

**Level 3: Manual Research**
- Specific compliment or insight
- Referenced recent post/project
- Shows genuine interest

## Writing Tips

- Write at 5th-8th grade reading level
- One sentence per line (mobile-friendly)
- No attachments on first email
- No images (deliverability)
- Avoid spam words: free, guarantee, no obligation, act now

## Metrics to Track

- **Open rate:** 30%+ (subject line test)
- **Reply rate:** 5-10% (target)
- **Meeting booked rate:** 1-3%
- **Bounce rate:** <5%

## Cold Email Template

```
Subject: [Company] + [Your Company]

Hi [Name],

[Personalized opening - specific observation].

[Problem statement relevant to their role/company].

[How you solved it for similar company - specific result].

[Worth a brief conversation?]

[Your name]
```
