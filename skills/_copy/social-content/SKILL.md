# Social Content

Create social media content that engages audiences and builds brand presence.

## When to Use
- Organic social media content
- Community building
- Brand awareness campaigns
- Thought leadership
- Customer engagement

## Don't Use When
- You need direct response/conversion (use ads)
- The content is purely promotional
- You don't have resources to engage with comments

## Platform-Specific Content

### LinkedIn
**Best for:** B2B, thought leadership, recruiting

**High-performing formats:**
- Personal stories with lessons
- Contrarian opinions (with substance)
- Carousel posts (educational)
- Document posts (PDFs with value)
- Polls

**Writing tips:**
- Hook in first 2 lines (before "see more")
- Line breaks for readability
- Tag relevant people (sparingly)
- End with question to drive comments

### Twitter/X
**Best for:** Real-time, networking, trends

**High-performing formats:**
- Threads (5-10 tweets, strong hook)
- Hot takes (with nuance)
- Listicles
- "How I" and "How to" posts
- Quote tweets with added value

**Writing tips:**
- First tweet must stop the scroll
- One idea per tweet
- Use formatting (line breaks, emojis)
- End threads with CTA

### Instagram
**Best for:** Visual brands, B2C, lifestyle

**High-performing formats:**
- Carousels (educational or entertaining)
- Reels (trends, tips, behind-scenes)
- Stories (authentic, ephemeral)
- User-generated content

**Content pillars:**
- Educational (how-to, tips)
- Entertaining (memes, humor)
- Inspirational (success stories)
- Promotional (products, offers)

### TikTok
**Best for:** Reach, younger audiences, viral potential

**High-performing formats:**
- Educational (quick tips)
- Behind-the-scenes
- Trending sounds/challenges
- Day-in-the-life
- Storytelling

**Best practices:**
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Captions on screen (sound-off viewing)
- Post consistently (1-3x/day)
- Engage in comments immediately

## Content Pillars Framework

Create consistent content by defining 3-5 pillars:

**Example for SaaS:**
1. Product education (tips, features)
2. Industry insights (trends, predictions)
3. Customer stories (case studies, testimonials)
4. Company culture (behind-the-scenes, team)
5. Thought leadership (opinions, frameworks)

**Content mix:**
- 40% Educational
- 30% Engaging/Entertaining
- 20% Promotional
- 10% Personal/Behind-scenes

## Content Creation Process

### 1. Ideation
- Customer questions (support, sales)
- Industry news/trends
- Repurpose long-form content
- Competitor analysis (what's working)
- Community conversations

### 2. Creation
- Hook first (what stops the scroll)
- Deliver value fast
- Clear takeaway or CTA
- Visuals that support the message

### 3. Scheduling
- Batch create content
- Use scheduling tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later)
- Post at optimal times (test and adjust)

### 4. Engagement
- Respond to comments within 1 hour
- Engage with others' content (10-15 min/day)
- Share user-generated content
- Monitor mentions and DMs

## Hook Formulas

- "I [did something]. Here's what happened:"
- "Stop doing [common practice]. Do this instead:"
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this:"
- "[Number] [things] I wish I knew before [event]"
- "Unpopular opinion: [statement]"
- "[Number] signs you're [thing]:"

## Metrics to Track

**Engagement:**
- Likes, comments, shares, saves
- Engagement rate (engagement/followers)

**Reach:**
- Impressions
- Follower growth
- Profile visits

**Conversion:**
- Link clicks
- Website traffic from social
- Lead generation
