# Subtle Inline CTA Banner

**File:** `library/ctas/subtle-inline-banner.md`

---

## Source
Atlas Roofing Co — atlas-roof-guard.base44.app

---

## Best For
- Dark-themed websites
- Between sections where you want a soft nudge, not a hard sell
- Maintaining visual flow without interruption

---

## Vibe
Urgent but understated. Dark background that flows with surrounding sections. Text is clear benefit-driven statement. Arrow suggests action without demanding it.

---

## Visual Specs (from reference)
- **Background:** Dark charcoal/black (#1a1a1a or similar) - matches section theme
- **Text:** White, bold, 18-20px
- **Arrow:** Red accent (#e63946 or similar), thin line, right-pointing
- **Padding:** 20-24px vertical, 40-60px horizontal
- **Border radius:** 8-12px
- **Width:** Contained (not full bleed), centered

---

## Prompt Snippet

```
Between-section CTA banner:
- Dark background matching site theme
- White bold text: "[Benefit-driven headline]"
- Red arrow icon on right side
- Contained width, centered
- Rounded corners (8-12px)
- Use sparingly - every other section maximum
```

---

## When to Use
- Dark-themed sites where bright CTA banners would clash
- When you want to maintain visual rhythm
- Strategic placement - not every section
- Between services and gallery, or gallery and testimonials

---

## When NOT to Use
- Light-themed sites (would need different color treatment)
- When you need urgent action (this is too subtle)
- More than once per 2-3 sections
- If it interrupts a visual sequence
