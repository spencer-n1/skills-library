# Customer Research

Conduct voice-of-customer research to uncover the exact words customers use, their pain points, and buying triggers.

## When to Use
- Developing messaging or positioning
- Writing copy that converts
- Identifying new features or improvements
- Creating case studies
- Understanding the buying journey

## Don't Use When
- You already have recent, quality research
- You're looking for quantitative validation (use surveys)
- You need statistically significant data

## Research Methods

### Method 1: Customer Interviews
**Best for:** Deep insights, emotional language, buying triggers

**Questions to ask:**
1. What was happening in your business/life that led you to look for a solution?
2. What were you using before? What was frustrating about it?
3. How did you discover us?
4. What made you choose us over alternatives?
5. What was your biggest concern before buying?
6. What would you tell someone considering us?
7. What's the #1 outcome you've achieved?

**Recording:**
- Record and transcribe (with permission)
- Note exact phrases and emotional words
- Tag by theme (pain point, outcome, objection, etc.)

### Method 2: Review Mining
**Sources:** G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Amazon, App stores

**What to capture:**
- What they love (benefits in their words)
- What they hate (pain points)
- What they compare you to
- Their use case
- Specific outcomes/results

### Method 3: Support Ticket Analysis
**Best for:** Product pain points, feature requests

**Categories to track:**
- Top complaints
- Most requested features
- Confusion points
- Success stories

### Method 4: Sales Call Analysis
**Best for:** Objections, buying process, decision criteria

**Listen for:**
- Questions prospects ask
- Concerns raised
- How they describe their problem
- Why they didn't buy (if lost)

## Output: Research Synthesis

Create a living document with:
- **Customer quotes** (organized by theme)
- **Before/After states** (what changed)
- **Top pain points** (ranked by frequency/intensity)
- **Desired outcomes** (what they want to achieve)
- **Objections** (and how to address them)
- **Language patterns** (words they actually use)

## Using Research

1. **Copywriting** — Use exact customer quotes in headlines
2. **Messaging** — Lead with their #1 pain point
3. **Product** — Prioritize most-requested features
4. **Sales** — Prepare answers to top objections
