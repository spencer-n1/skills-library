# Marketing Ideas

Generate creative, strategic marketing campaign ideas that break through noise and drive results.

## When to Use
- Planning a new campaign or launch
- Stuck for creative angles
- Need fresh positioning
- Looking for viral/growth ideas
- Refreshing tired messaging

## Don't Use When
- You haven't defined your target audience
- You need executional tactics (use specific skills)
- The idea needs legal/compliance review first

## Idea Generation Frameworks

### Framework 1: Contrarian Positioning
**Core:** Take the opposite stance from industry norms

**Prompts:**
- What does everyone in our industry believe that's wrong?
- What "best practice" hurts more than helps?
- What can we credibly disagree with?

**Examples:**
- "Email is dead" → "Email is more alive than ever"
- "Hustle culture" → "Rest is productive"

### Framework 2: Category Creation
**Core:** Define a new category you can own

**Prompts:**
- What category are we *really* in?
- What problem do we solve that no one names?
- What would we call this if it didn't exist yet?

**Output:** Category name + problem frame + your solution

### Framework 3: The Uncomfortable Truth
**Core:** Say what customers think but no one admits

**Prompts:**
- What are customers secretly frustrated by?
- What do they wish they could say?
- What truth are competitors ignoring?

### Framework 4: Borrowed Authority
**Core:** Use familiar frameworks/concepts as analogies

**Examples:**
- "The [X] for [Y]" (Uber for X, Netflix for Y)
- Apply scientific/psychology concepts to business
- Use pop culture references strategically

### Framework 5: The Manifesto
**Core:** Take a bold stand on a cultural/trend issue

**Prompts:**
- What do we believe that others don't?
- What change are we fighting for?
- What hill would we die on?

## Campaign Types

| Type | Best For | Example Angles |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **Educational** | Complex products, trust building | How-to series, myth-busting, frameworks |
| **Challenger** | Disrupting incumbents | Vs campaigns, why we're different, industry critiques |
| **Community** | B2B, mission-driven brands | Customer stories, behind-the-scenes, user-generated content |
| **Viral/Growth** | Consumer, low-friction products | Referral programs, gamification, controversy |
| **Authority** | High-ticket, trust-required | Research reports, original data, expert content |

## Evaluation Criteria

Rate each idea on:
1. **Resonance** — Will our audience care?
2. **Differentiation** — Can competitors copy it easily?
3. **Resource fit** — Can we execute with our team/budget?
4. **Strategic alignment** — Does it support business goals?
5. **Scalability** — Can it grow or be repeated?

## Output Format

For each selected idea:
- **Concept** (1 sentence)
- **Angle/Hook** (what makes it interesting)
- **Format** (content type, channel)
- **Success metric**
- **Resources needed**
- **Timeline**
