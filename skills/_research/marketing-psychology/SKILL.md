# Marketing Psychology

Apply proven behavioral psychology principles to increase conversion and engagement.

## When to Use
- Writing sales copy
- Designing landing pages
- Creating pricing strategies
- Building email sequences
- Optimizing CTAs

## Core Principles

### 1. Loss Aversion
**Concept:** Losses feel ~2x as painful as equivalent gains feel good.

**Applications:**
- Frame benefits as "avoid losing" rather than "gain"
- "Don't miss out" vs "Get this"
- Show what they lose by not acting
- Free trial with data they'll lose if they cancel

### 2. Social Proof
**Concept:** People look to others' behavior to guide their own.

**Types:**
- **Expert** — Endorsements from authorities
- **Celebrity** — Well-known figures
- **User** — Customer reviews, testimonials
- **Wisdom of crowds** — "Join 10,000+ customers"
- **Friends** — Referrals, "your friend bought this"

**Best practices:**
- Use specific numbers over round ones
- Include photos/names for credibility
- Show people *like* the prospect
- Update social proof regularly

### 3. Scarcity & Urgency
**Concept:** Limited availability increases perceived value.

**Types:**
- **Quantity scarcity** — "Only 3 left"
- **Time scarcity** — "Sale ends in 4 hours"
- **Access scarcity** — Waitlists, limited spots

**Critical:** Must be genuine or you'll destroy trust

### 4. Reciprocity
**Concept:** People feel obligated to return favors.

**Applications:**
- Free valuable content (lead magnets)
- Free tools or calculators
- Generous free trials
- Unexpected bonuses

### 5. Commitment & Consistency
**Concept:** People want to act consistently with their past behavior.

**Applications:**
- Small commitments lead to bigger ones
- Public commitments are stronger
- Get them to say "yes" to small things first
- Progress bars for onboarding

### 6. Authority
**Concept:** People defer to experts and credentials.

**Applications:**
- Display credentials prominently
- Use expert testimonials
- Cite research and data
- Guest experts in content

### 7. The Endowment Effect
**Concept:** People value things more once they own them.

**Applications:**
- Free trials (they "own" it temporarily)
- "Your" account/dashboard language
- Customization increases ownership
- "Choose your plan" vs "Sign up"

### 8. Anchoring
**Concept:** First number encountered influences subsequent judgments.

**Applications:**
- Show higher price first (or crossed out)
- Enterprise plan anchors other tiers
- "Worth $X, yours for $Y"
- Comparison to expensive alternatives

### 9. The Paradox of Choice
**Concept:** Too many options reduce decision-making.

**Applications:**
- Limit plan options (3 is ideal)
- Highlight a "most popular" choice
- Default selections
- Clear comparison between options

### 10. The IKEA Effect
**Concept:** People value things they partially created.

**Applications:**
- DIY setup/onboarding
- Customizable templates
- Co-creation with customers
- Input into product roadmap

## Using This Skill

Don't manipulate — use psychology to:
- Help people make decisions faster
- Communicate value more clearly
- Reduce friction in the buying process
- Build genuine trust and credibility
